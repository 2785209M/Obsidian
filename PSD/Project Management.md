# Agile
The core concept of agile is to prioritize individuals and interactions, and break projects down into sprints.
## Retrospectives and Sprint Reviews
The purpose or a retrospective is to reflect on what went well and what went poorly with people and tools during a sprint, and to plan changes accordingly. The key difference is that a sprint review focuses on the product and a sprint retrospective focuses on the process. They are used synergistically to improve overall team processes.

## User Stories
### INVEST criteria
**Independent** - Stories should not rely on other stories to be complete
**Negotiable** - Stories should not be rigid contracts. They should invite conversation between the product owner and development team.
**Valuable** - Each story must provide clear value to the end user or business
**Estimable** - the team must have enough information to estimate the story's size for planning purposes
**Small** - Stories should be small enough to fit within a single sprint iteration, typically manageable within a few days
**Testable** - Stories require clear acceptance criteria to verify they have been successfully implemented

# Patterns
The difference between architectural patterns and design patterns is scope and abstraction.
Architectural patterns are like designing a city, whilst design patterns are used for designing houses.
## Design Patterns
Design patterns are low level solutions to specific problems encountered in a specific component.
Focus: Code re-usability, maintainability, and solving technical problems
Easier to refactor or change than overall architecture
**Examples:**
**Singleton:** every class has only one instance
**Observer:** A way for an object to notify other objects of state changes
**Strategy:** Allowing an algorithm's behavior to be selected at runtime
